2. u/SuspiciouslyDullGuy [score=1, created=2025-11-10T04:03:53]: The Dexcom One+ (and G7) jumps around a bit for the first 24 hours+ before settling down. The basic accuracy standards for these devices leave quite a bit of wiggle room. Once it settles and after calibrating with a very good finger stick meter (I use a Contour Next One for this) I typically get 8 or 9 days worth of accurate readings per sensor, though I often find I have to recalibrate it every couple of days if I want high accuracy. I put the calibration drift down to changes in hydration though I'm unsure what causes it. Other factors can affect it temporarily though, such as compression lows at night which happen due to pressure on the sensor if you lie on top of it, and temperature changes such as going out in cold weather or having a hot shower. If it's still giving you inaccurate readings after a couple of days wear and calibration doesn't fix it you can often get a free replacement sensor, depending on how far off the mark the CGM readings are.
