4. u/VeniABE [score=1, created=2025-11-15T01:17:47]: From what I read in the research, blaming things on poor habits is a bit of an oversimplification. There are a lot of things that might make those habits fine for 9 in 10 people but push another person over the edge. Generally the information you find about people testing foods is done with a 50 gram dose of carbohydrates. I would suggest trying to stay much lower until you get a monitor. The long term goal is to keep blood sugar below 130 almost all the time. It will be normal to spend a lot of time above that at first. My biggest success was finding a way to make pasta dishes without raising my blood sugar levels above 120. There are tricks.
