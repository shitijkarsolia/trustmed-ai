1. u/Major_Evidence_7850 [score=2, created=2025-11-06T15:47:39]: Everyone on here does different things. For me I had to find what works best for me. I had to see nourishing my body verses taking away. I had to find ways to cook even if I was tired and didn't want to. I would come up with a list of foods that aren't hard to make. Right now you need to make sure you are eating more. Focus on getting more protein not just from nuts and peanut butter but eggs, chicken, tuna in moderation other forms of meat or protein products. I focus on veggies at most meals then eating protein then eating fats and carbs in that order. If you are going to have dessert eat it right after dinner and then do calf raises of go for a short walk it can help with the spikes. Eat half the dessert and don't let it become and everyday thing. I know ADHD can cause all or nothing thinking but I would work on that. If you eat one cookie you dont have to freak out and sabatoge. I stopped labeling food as good or bad. I stopped placing so much value on food. I started really small with excercise and worked my way up slowly. Had to find what works for me. Try to not be rigid and take small steps often. Do t jump into restriction causes more issues especially with insulin issues and even leads to binging. I can't do low carb doesn't work for me. Some people get continuous glucose monitors which shows what food spikes you. They aren't through insurance though. It's a lot and can be overwhelming. Start small and find what works for you. There is a lot of noise and I had to be mindful about not becoming obsessive.
