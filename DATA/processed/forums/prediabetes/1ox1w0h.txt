I was diagnosed as pre-diabetic in October 2024 with A1C at 5.7. I kinda ignored it and didn't take it seriously. Did another blood test as part of routine annual exam in May 2025 and my A1C was at 5.9. That concerned me and I started making some changes to my lifestyle. I wanted to make lifestyle changes instead of making changes that I can't stick to. I got 3 months supply of Stelo CGM and started monitoring how my body reacts to food I eat. Reduced carbs (missed rice :-() and increased protein intake. Use apps like Cronometer, GlucospikeAI to keep track of what I am eating and macro nutrients. Not a gym person but started hitting gym about 2-3 times a week doing strength training. There are weeks where I was not consistent. :-( but pushed myself to go to gym. If I can get to the gym, I really enjoyed the workout and how my body felt afterwards but its the fact that I needed to get off the couch which was a problem. Tried to sleep better but failed miserably because of my non-stop work travel. I sleep well when I am home which is about 50% of the time. Was able to manage stress overall. Here is the bad part - As far as food good, I ended up taking too much nuts such as almonds, walnuts and cashews. I also had avacado almost everyday. I did a blood test this week, the good news is that my A1c is at 5.3!! Yay!! But my LDL, non-hdl cholesterol, total cholesterol shot up. Interestingly, my HDL is all time high as well. Met with my doc this morning - she mentioned that my ASCVD (cardiac) risk score has increased and its at 5. Never heard of this before. She advised me to adjust my diet to keep ldl and cholesterol in control. Recommended to do another blood work in 3 months to see where I stand - she mentioned if the ldl and total cholesterol come back in range, she will have to prescribe cholesterol medication - which I am not a big fan of. Looks like I need to start thinking about moderation of my cholesterol intake. Sharing as others could benefit from my experience. Will report back in months.
