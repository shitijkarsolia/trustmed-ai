3. u/egg1st [score=3, created=2025-09-05T23:55:18]: I'm fortunate that it was detected in me early and I've been able to get my AC1 below 6.5% with only a small dose of Metformin, diet and exercise. At it's worst I've had slight tingling in my fingers and toes, and I've had a couple of hypers, both after too much alcohol the previous night. Given your situation, I would suggest you read up on the disease and learn about all the horrible shit it does to you. Understand it, decide how you're going to tackle it and apply yourself. When I was diagnosed, I set myself two simple aims based on what I had read about controlling T2. Manage my carbs day to day to control my current blood sugar and lose weight/body fat over time to give my pancreas a chance to recover. I've lost 10kg and it seems to have been enough to allow my pancreas to produce enough insulin to mean I can have a more normal diet, albeit weary of carbs and avoiding straight sugar. I know I haven't won, I'll always need to keep things in check, but the strategy worked to give me more freedom of choice.
