1. u/Zeus783 [score=2, created=2025-10-06T10:43:20]: The amount of carbs one can tolerate varies from person to person. What I can handle another wouldn't and the other way around. The 'good amount of carbs' is the one that her glucose meter says it's good for her. T2 is a very personal disease ; there are some guidelines that applies to everyone (i.e. Keep carbs low to avoid spikes) but most of it is down to the individual... Hence there isn't one pill that fixes it an why sometime people have to try various medications until they find what works for them. I assume she has a glucose meter? As she should eat..test.. adjust portions for next meals if she's gone too high in her spikes. For most people 100/150 gr of carbs per day (some even per meal) is too high...whilst other can handle that amount quite well with the medications they're taking. It sounds messy at first.. To be fair it probably is.. Until you get the gist of it and things start to make sense, at which point one just need good will power and things will be under control. All the best! üëç
