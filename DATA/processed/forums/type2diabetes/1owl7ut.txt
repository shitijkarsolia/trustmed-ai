3. u/hu_gnew [score=3, created=2025-11-14T00:52:55]: The net vs total carb thing can stir up a storm. The problem is some things that count as fiber on a nutrition label can in fact make your blood glucose rise depending on how your body reacts to them. Two of such are modified starches and resistant starches. In my case modified starches will give me a very large spike while resistant starches don't have that affect if consumed in appropriate amounts. A lot of products that tout zero or low net carbs use these ingredients and they'll be listed 1st or 2nd. The way I deal with this is by avoiding modified starches entirely and making sure I deduct the total carbs in things with resistant starches from my daily carb budget. When listed I do deduct non-soluable fiber to calculate a figure for net carbs. The details on nutrition labels can be very helpful when eating mindfully and with purpose.
