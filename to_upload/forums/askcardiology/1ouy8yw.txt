1. u/Impressive_Donut3122 [score=1, created=2025-11-12T08:43:33]: PCP is correct, lead misplacement is actually a very common reason for false ECG findings. Even small placement errors can make the P wave look larger or change its shape, which can be misread as enlargement when the heart is actually normal.
2. u/HydroxFrost [score=1, created=2025-11-14T09:22:27]: Sounds like poor lead placement, if you're concerned about enlargement it needs to be diagnosed via echocardiogram.
