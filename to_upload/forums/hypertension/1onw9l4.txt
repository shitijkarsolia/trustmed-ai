## Comments
1. u/Ajay_Hellohealth [score=3, created=2025-11-05T01:51:27]: there isn’t one single “lowest dose” because different blood pressure meds are different strengths, but in general doctors usually start on very small beginner doses. like for example amlodipine often starts at 2.5–5 mg, losartan often starts at 25 mg, telmisartan around 20 mg, enalapril sometimes 2.5–5 mg. the numbers look different because the drugs are measured differently, not because one is stronger than another. the idea is start small, see how your body responds, then adjust slowly. the goal is to lower BP gently, not drop it too fast.
2. u/edumedibw [score=1, created=2025-11-04T00:12:49]: All cause a similar 10mmhg reduction at the standard dose. You could try 1.25mg!
