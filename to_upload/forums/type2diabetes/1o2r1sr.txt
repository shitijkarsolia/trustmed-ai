Quick primer: 54-year-old male, was in pre-diabetic territory until blood test in July came back with a high fasting blood sugar (282), and a couple liver numbers also came back high. My doctor put me on metformin, and I have lost 15 pounds since (down at least 33 in last 18 months) and am eating smarter, cutting down on carbs and sugar. I bought a blood sugar meter and my numbers have looked good -- mostly in a non-diabetic range, and I've seen only a couple spikes over 200, and nothing higher than 221 -- and I'm getting an A1C test next week. I was laid off in May, and my doctor said my system might have went into warrior mode from the stress. Here's my question: Is anyone enrolled in the Teledoc diabetes management program? It's apparently a benefit of my health insurance, and I got something in the mail saying I'm eligible and can receive a free meter, strips, and lancets, as well as an app to track everything. It sounds legit, but I'm skeptical it's not Teladoc trying to market more of its services or get my health data, or that it's my usually good insurance trying to nudge me toward telehealth visits. Cynical? Yeah, perhaps, but I'm not sure free strips is worth whatever tradeoff (if any) is expected
