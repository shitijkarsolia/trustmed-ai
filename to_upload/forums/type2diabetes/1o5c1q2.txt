1. u/moronmonday526 [score=1, created=2025-10-13T07:41:49]: Your expectations may need adjusting if you're looking for a CGM that has "barely any issues" at a bare minimum. As the saying goes, "No one goes online to say everything is perfect." That is very true in the world of CGMs. That said, congratulations on the progress you've made so far. My A1c dropped from 13.4 to 7.3 on 2,000 mg/day of Metformin. I got it down to 6.8 after consulting with a nutritionist. Once I started using a CGM, I was able to get off my meds and drop my A1c down to 5.8. Has it been perfect? Of course not. Has it changed my life for the better? Absolutely. I calibrate my G7 with one fingerstick 12 hours after installation, or once every 10 days. Oh, and the Stelo is not for you once you start taking insulin. It is for non-insulin-dependent diabetics.
